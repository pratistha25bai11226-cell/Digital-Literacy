# Social Media Do's and Don'ts for College Students
### A Responsible Digital Presence Checklist

---

## ✅ DO's

| # | Do | Why It Matters |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | **Think before you post** — ask yourself if you'd be comfortable with a future employer or professor seeing it. | Your digital footprint is long-lasting and publicly searchable. |
| 2 | **Use privacy settings** on all your accounts to control who can see your posts, photos, and personal information. | Protects you from strangers, data scrapers, and unwanted attention. |
| 3 | **Give credit** when you share someone else's content, artwork, writing, or ideas. | Respects intellectual property and builds trust in your community. |
| 4 | **Verify information before sharing** — check at least two credible sources before reposting news or claims. | Sharing misinformation, even unintentionally, damages your credibility. |
| 5 | **Keep your professional profiles (LinkedIn, GitHub) up to date** and separate from casual social profiles. | Recruiters and internship coordinators actively search candidates online. |
| 6 | **Use a respectful and measured tone** when engaging in debates, disagreements, or sensitive topics online. | Constructive dialogue builds your reputation; aggression destroys it. |
| 7 | **Log out of shared or public devices** after using social media or any personal accounts. | Prevents unauthorised access to your accounts and personal data. |

---

## ❌ DON'Ts

| # | Don't | Why It's Harmful |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | **Don't post offensive, discriminatory, or hateful content** — even as a "joke" or in a private group. | Screenshots travel. This can lead to academic disciplinary action or job rejections. |
| 2 | **Don't share personal details publicly** such as your address, phone number, daily schedule, or ID numbers. | Makes you vulnerable to stalking, identity theft, and social engineering attacks. |
| 3 | **Don't engage in or ignore cyberbullying** — neither participating in nor staying silent is acceptable. | Both actions normalise harm; report and block instead. |
| 4 | **Don't accept friend or follow requests from strangers** without verifying their identity, especially on professional platforms. | Fake profiles are commonly used for phishing, scamming, or data harvesting. |
| 5 | **Don't vent about your college, professors, classmates, or workplace** on public social media. | Even vague posts can be identified, screenshotted, and shared out of context. |
| 6 | **Don't use the same password across multiple social media accounts.** | A single breach can compromise all your accounts simultaneously. |
| 7 | **Don't post or forward unverified news, rumours, or health/safety alerts** without fact-checking. | Misinformation spreads faster than corrections and causes real-world harm. |
