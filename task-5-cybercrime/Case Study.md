# Case Study – UPI / Online Payment Fraud
### Task 5 (Part A) | Digital Ambassador Program

---

## Crime Type: UPI / Online Payment Fraud

---

## What Is It?

UPI (Unified Payments Interface) fraud is a form of financial cybercrime in which attackers manipulate victims into authorising money transfers from their own bank accounts — often without the victim realising they are sending money rather than receiving it. It is one of the fastest-growing cybercrimes in India, particularly targeting first-time UPI users, students, and rural populations.

---

## Realistic Case Scenario: "The OLX Scam"

**Victim:** Rohan, a 20-year-old engineering student in Bhopal.

Rohan listed his old laptop for ₹18,000 on OLX. Within hours, he received a WhatsApp message from someone claiming to be an Army officer stationed in Pune, interested in buying the laptop urgently.

**Step-by-step breakdown of how the fraud unfolded:**

1. **Establishing trust:** The caller sent a fake Indian Army ID card and spoke confidently, creating an air of authority and legitimacy.

2. **Creating urgency:** He said he needed the laptop before a posting transfer and insisted on paying via UPI immediately.

3. **The trap — a collect request:** The fraudster sent Rohan a ₹18,000 *collect request* on Google Pay. He told Rohan, *"Just enter your UPI PIN to receive the money."*

4. **The victim authorises the payment:** Rohan, unfamiliar with the difference between a payment request and a receive request, entered his PIN — and ₹18,000 was debited from his account, not credited.

5. **Disappearance:** The fraudster immediately blocked Rohan on WhatsApp and became unreachable.

---

## Who Is Targeted?

- College students selling items on OLX, Facebook Marketplace, or Quikr
- People receiving money for the first time via UPI
- Senior citizens unfamiliar with digital payment flows
- Job seekers promised "registration fees refunded via UPI"
- Individuals responding to fake cashback or KYC update messages

---

## Consequences

| Impact | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Financial loss** | Rohan lost ₹18,000 with no immediate recovery mechanism |
| **Psychological impact** | Shame, self-blame, and reluctance to report |
| **Legal complexity** | UPI transactions are difficult to reverse once authorised by the user |
| **Data exposure** | Victims who click on fraudulent links also risk losing login credentials |

---

## Key Takeaway

> **A UPI PIN is only for sending money — never for receiving it.**
> If anyone asks you to enter your PIN or scan a QR code to *receive* a payment, it is fraud. Stop immediately.
