# Stay Safe Online – Prevention Checklist
### For College Students in India | Task 5 (Part B)

---

> 🚨 **Report cybercrime immediately at:**
> - 🌐 **cybercrime.gov.in** — National Cyber Crime Reporting Portal
> - 📞 **Helpline: 1930** — National Cybercrime Helpline (24×7)

---

## ✅ General Online Safety

- [ ] **1. Use strong, unique passwords for every account.**
  Create passwords with a mix of uppercase, lowercase, numbers, and symbols (e.g., `Rh@n#2025!`). Never reuse the same password across multiple platforms. Use a password manager like Bitwarden or Google Password Manager if needed.

- [ ] **2. Enable Two-Factor Authentication (2FA) on all important accounts.**
  Turn on 2FA for Gmail, Instagram, WhatsApp, banking apps, and college portals. This adds a second layer of protection even if your password is stolen.

- [ ] **3. Never click on suspicious links in SMS, WhatsApp, or email.**
  Fraudsters often disguise links as bank alerts, parcel tracking notifications, or scholarship forms. Always type the official URL directly into your browser instead of clicking embedded links.

- [ ] **4. Verify the identity of anyone asking for personal information online.**
  No legitimate bank, government body, or employer will ask for your Aadhaar number, OTP, password, or PIN via WhatsApp, SMS, or phone call. Hang up or ignore such requests.

- [ ] **5. Check app permissions before installing any application.**
  Avoid apps that request access to contacts, SMS, or microphone when those permissions are not required for the app's function. Download apps only from the Google Play Store or Apple App Store.

- [ ] **6. Do not use public Wi-Fi (cafes, malls, buses) for sensitive activities.**
  Public networks can be monitored. Avoid logging into banking apps, college portals, or email accounts over unsecured public Wi-Fi. Use mobile data or a trusted VPN instead.

---

## 💳 UPI & Financial Safety (Mandatory)

- [ ] **7. Remember: A UPI PIN is ONLY for sending money — never for receiving it.**
  If someone asks you to enter your UPI PIN, scan a QR code, or approve a "collect request" in order to *receive* money — it is 100% a scam. Legitimate payments arrive automatically with no action required from the recipient.

- [ ] **8. Never share OTPs with anyone, under any circumstances.**
  Your bank, UPI app (PhonePe, GPay, Paytm), or NPCI will never call you and ask for an OTP. An OTP shared is an account compromised. End the call immediately and report it to your bank.

- [ ] **9. Set a daily UPI transaction limit in your banking app.**
  Most bank apps allow you to set a per-day UPI transfer limit. Keeping it low (e.g., ₹5,000/day unless needed) minimises potential loss if your account is ever compromised.

- [ ] **10. Always double-check the recipient's UPI ID or QR code before confirming a payment.**
  Fraudsters create UPI IDs and QR codes that look almost identical to legitimate ones (e.g., `amazon-support@ybl` instead of a verified merchant handle). Verify the name shown on the payment confirmation screen before entering your PIN.

---

## 🔐 Account & Device Safety

- [ ] **11. Lock your phone with a PIN, pattern, or biometric, and enable remote wipe.**
  If your phone is stolen and unlocked, all your UPI apps, email, and social media are instantly accessible. Enable "Find My Device" on Android or "Find My iPhone" on iOS.

- [ ] **12. Regularly update your phone's OS and all apps.**
  Outdated software contains security vulnerabilities that fraudsters actively exploit. Enable automatic updates wherever possible.

---

## 📢 If You Are Victimised

| Step | Action |
|---|---|
| **1. Don't panic** | Act quickly but calmly — early reporting increases the chance of freezing the fraudulent transaction |
| **2. Call 1930 immediately** | The National Cybercrime Helpline can help initiate a bank hold on the recipient account |
| **3. File a complaint online** | Visit **cybercrime.gov.in** and submit a detailed complaint with transaction IDs, screenshots, and phone numbers |
| **4. Inform your bank** | Call your bank's 24×7 helpline and request a fraud investigation |
| **5. Preserve all evidence** | Do not delete messages, call logs, or screenshots — these are required for the complaint |
